## What Verity Protocol is

Verity Protocol is a public, permissionless registry for real-world
assets. Anyone with an Ethereum wallet can list an asset by signing a
canonical message. The signature and details are stored on Verity's
servers, and a bound B20 Asset token is minted on Base to the signing
wallet as an immutable record of the claim.

Every listing is a claim made by the wallet that signed it. No entity
vouches for the truth of any claim; Verity operates the platform, not
the assets.

## What Verity Protocol is not

- **Not a security.** B20 Asset tokens are non-transferable from mint.
  They cannot be bought, sold, traded, or fractionally split.
- **Not verification.** Verity does not verify that an asset exists,
  that a registrant owns it, or that any documentation is authentic.
- **Not custody.** Verity never holds, stores, moves, or has legal
  access to the underlying asset.
- **Not a marketplace.** Verity does not match buyers with sellers,
  does not escrow funds, and does not clear transactions.
- **Not legal advice.** A Verity registration does not replace the
  deed, contract, license, or other legal instrument governing the
  asset in your jurisdiction.

## Registrant responsibilities

- The information you sign is accurate to the best of your knowledge,
  and you have the right to publish it.
- The wallet you sign with is under your control.
- Your registration is public and permanent. Signed records cannot be
  edited or erased once recorded.
- Documentation URLs are public. Do not link private files.

## Reader responsibilities

- Every listing is a self-attestation. Verity does not underwrite,
  insure, or guarantee any listing.
- Endorsements, verifier badges, evidence tiers, AI plausibility
  reviews, and conflict-detection signals are informational aids.
- Do your own verification against the underlying asset and its
  off-chain legal framework before acting.
- Verity assumes no liability for actions you take in reliance on the
  registry.

## Verifier declarations

Wallets can publicly declare themselves as professional verifiers.
Declarations are permissionless and not vetted by Verity. Readers
evaluate each verifier by the proof URL they publish.

## Privacy

- Listings and their metadata are public.
- Documentation you attach is stored on Verity's servers and served
  publicly.
- Partnership inquiries submitted via the /partners form are stored
  privately.
- We do not collect or resell personal information beyond what you
  voluntarily provide.

## Content moderation

Verity does not moderate the content of signed claims. Where a listing
violates applicable law or the rights of a third party, Verity may
hide the listing from public view; the underlying signed record and
its B20 token remain.

## Fees

Registering an asset is free. Mints on Base cost the network's standard
gas fee, paid to Base validators. Certain roles (verifiers, institutional
API customers) may involve paid subscriptions or contracts in the
future.
